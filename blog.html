Important FAQ

What is Flood?

A flood is an overflow of water that submerges land that is usually dry. Floods can occur for various reasons, including heavy rainfall, storm surges from tropical cyclones, melting snow, or dam failures. They are among the most common natural disasters and can be highly destructive, leading to loss of life, property damage, and displacement of people.

Key Causes of Flooding in Bangladesh:

Bangladesh experiences a tropical monsoon climate, with heavy rainfall occurring during the monsoon season (June to October). This can overwhelm the natural drainage systems and cause rivers to overflow. The Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers, which converge in Bangladesh, drain a massive watershed that spans several countries. During the monsoon, these rivers swell due to upstream precipitation in India, Nepal, and China, leading to widespread inundation in the downstream floodplains of Bangladesh.

Immediate Response and Relief During Floods

TImplementing and improving early warning systems can give communities time to prepare and evacuate. Alerts for heavy rainfall, river overflow, or potential storm surges help reduce casualties and property loss. Predefined evacuation routes and shelters need to be well-publicized and ready for use during floods. Having an organized evacuation process reduces chaos and ensures people's safety.

When flood occur in Bangladesh?

Floods in Bangladesh typically occur during the monsoon season, which lasts from June to October. This period is characterized by heavy rainfall, both in Bangladesh and in the upstream river basins of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. These rivers flow from countries like India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China, contributing to flooding in Bangladesh when they overflow.
